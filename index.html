Kinticky

The open-source autonomous marine observation platform

Kinticky is a highly configurable platform for marine research, remote sensing and maritime security missions.

Learn more »

What is this all about?

We're building a custom-engineered, solar-powered autonomous boat. We're in the prototyping phase for the hardware, electronics and software.

We're designing it to last years out at sea, collecting data, weathering storms, accepting new missions all using mostly off-the-shelf parts.

It's solar powered and uses a magnetically-coupled drive system to allow it to move quickly to its intended destination.

If you think it'd be helpful in your research projects, give us a shout. The project is currently in development

Who are you guys?

Two geeks and friends in Silicon Valley. One of us is an aeronautical engineer from MIT, one is a seasoned software developer who loves autonomous vehicles and solving real problems.

Where are all the pictures?

What are you working on right now?

Magnetically-coupled drive
We've got a prototype, but we're working on building a good test rig. We need to instrument the drive to test speed, torque, thrust, durability, etc. to make sure it's performing up to spec.
Navigation system
Getting a prototype of the navigation system on the water and running it through its paces.

How soon will you be ready?

We're planning on launching Kinticky on its maiden voyage in January 2015. It'll head from the San Fransisco Bay area to Hawaii where we'll evaluate it and send it back.
